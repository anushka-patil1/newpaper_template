Mumbai Traffic Police announces Gudi Padwa 2024 restrictions: Here are some routes that might be affected

The Mumbai Traffic Police has announced restrictions on several South Bombay routes in anticipation of the large inflow of people celebrating Gudi Padwa 2024. Gudi Padwa, marking the onset of spring and warmer days, also signifies the beginning of the Marathi New Year, celebrated with much pomp and show. As people prepare for rallies, here are the traffic restrictions to avoid congestion. It is advised to check traffic restrictions to avoid inconvenience.Marking the beginning of the traditional new year, Gudi Padwa is primarily celebrated by the people in Maharashtra and Goa. The auspicious festival derives its name from two words -- 'gudi' which is the flag of Lord Brahma and 'padwa', which signifies the first day of the phase of the moon. The occasion signals the onset of warmer days and the spring season. The festival is observed with colourful floor decorations, a special Gudhi flag garlanded with flowers, mango and neem leaves, and topped with upturned silver or copper vessels, street processions, dancing, and festive foods like shrikhand puri. Gudi Padwa coincides with the first day of the Chaitra Navratri and festivals like Sajibu Cheiraoba, Navreh, Cheti Chand and Ugadi that are celebrated in different parts of the country. Prime Minister Narendra Modi and Governor of Maharashtra Ramesh Bais have extended their greetings to the nation on the occasion of Gudi Padwa 2024. In a message, Governor Bais said, "I extend my heartiest greetings to all on the occasion of Gudi Padwa and the commencement of the New Year."

Ghaziabad (UP): Three persons, including a couple, have been run over by a speeding train while they were "making videos with mobile phones" on a railway track here, police said on Thursday. Andhra Pradesh girl tops intermediate exams, despite child marriage pressure

Despite her parents' plans for a child marriage, G. Nirmala from Pedda Harivanam, Kurnool district, Andhra Pradesh, achieved a top position in the AP intermediate board exams 2024.

A young girl, once on the brink of a forced marriage, has defied all obstacles and emerged as the top scorer in this year's intermediate examinations in Andhra Pradesh. Her journey is one of remarkable courage and determination in the face of adversity. Hailing from Pedda Harivanam in Kurnool district's Adoni mandal, her determination and resilience have been her guiding forces. Despite her impoverished background and the pressure from her parents, who had already married off her three sisters, Nirmala refused to give up on her education. When her parents sought to end her schooling due to financial constraints and the lack of a nearby junior college, Nirmala sought help from local legislator Y Saiprasad Reddy during his outreach programme.
